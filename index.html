About

Larry Nelson, Everybody!

Larry Nelson is a graduate of Simon Fraser University and a chartered accountant. He worked in industry and public practice for many years before eventually transitioning into the not-for-profit sector. Active in local politics, he was elected to the Coquitlam school board and served as a trustee for three terms.

A lifelong interest in affordable housing led to his work with several not-for-profit organizations – the BC Non-Profit Housing Association, Monarch Place Transitional Housing for women with children fleeing domestic abuse, and the New Hope Community Services Society, which provided refugee housing.

For several years Larry served as board chair for the Canadian Council of Christian Charities and currently serves on the leadership advisory council for Trinity Western University's School of Business. Larry worked with the Baptist Housing Ministries Society for 13 years as its chief executive officer and retired from that position in 2007. During his association with the organization Larry assisted in the creation of retiremet housing for more than 1,800 seniors in comunities throughout BC. Over the years Larry has served on more than ten not-for-profit boards, many of them as board chair. He is currently vice chair of the board of the Terminal City Club in Vancouver.

Larry is currently a senior associate with the executive search firm Hutchinson Group, Inc., which focuses on placing senior leaders in the not-for-profit sector and has offices in Vancouver, Toronto and Ottawa. He also assists charities in conducting organization, policy and program reviews and board governance consulting in the areas of strategic planning, best board practices and succession planning.
